Word Counter FAQs

What is a word counter?

A word counter is an online tool that analyzes your text and provides detailed statistics including word count, character count (with and without spaces), sentence count, paragraph count, and estimated reading time. It's essential for writers, students, bloggers, and professionals who need to meet specific word limits or track their writing progress.

How does this word counter calculate words and characters?

Our word counter uses advanced JavaScript algorithms to analyze your text in real-time. Words are counted by splitting the text on whitespace characters, characters are counted including all letters, numbers, symbols, and spaces (with a separate count excluding spaces), sentences are identified by punctuation marks (periods, exclamation marks, question marks), and paragraphs are counted by detecting double line breaks. The tool updates automatically as you type, providing instant feedback.

Is my text saved or uploaded anywhere?

No, absolutely not. Your text is never saved, uploaded, or transmitted to any server. All processing happens entirely locally in your browser using JavaScript. Your privacy is our top priority, and we don't collect, store, or share any of your data. You can use this tool with complete confidence that your content remains private.

How accurate is the reading time?

The reading time is an estimate based on an average reading speed of 200 words per minute, which is the standard reading speed for adults. Actual reading time may vary depending on the complexity of the text, the reader's individual speed, and their familiarity with the topic. It's provided as a helpful guideline to give you an approximate idea of how long it might take someone to read your content.

Can I download my text?

Yes! You can download your text as a .txt file using the "Download Text" button. This allows you to save your work locally on your device. The downloaded file will contain exactly what you've typed in the text area.

Does the tool work offline?

Once the page is loaded, the word counter functionality works entirely offline. Since all processing is done in your browser using JavaScript, you don't need an active internet connection to use the tool after the initial page load. However, you'll need internet to first load the page and its resources.
